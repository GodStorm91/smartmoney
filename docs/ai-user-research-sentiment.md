# User Research & Sentiment: What Users Actually Want

**Data sources:** Reddit, NerdWallet, App Store reviews, fintech blogs (2025-2026)

## Top Unmet Needs (Ranked by Frequency)

### 1. Security & Privacy (24% of mentions)
- Bank-level encryption, transparent data handling
- No third-party aggregators (anti-Plaid sentiment)
- Clear data deletion + export capabilities
- "Use local-first apps; don't trust cloud fintech"

### 2. Real-Time Automation (22%)
- Auto-categorization that learns
- Dynamic budget adjustment (income-aware)
- Instant alerts on overspend/unusual activity
- "Don't make me do manual work"

### 3. Customization (18%)
- Modify categories (not one-size-fits-all)
- Custom reports, flexible budgeting
- Category aliases ("Starbucks" = "Coffee")
- "Apps should fit my life, not vice versa"

### 4. Forecasting (16%)
- Predict shortfalls, cash position next month
- Income-aware budgets (irregular income)
- "Will I have enough for rent?"

### 5. Mobile UX (12%)
- Minimal taps, distraction-free design
- Fast transaction logging (voice capture ideal)
- Dark mode, accessibility
- Offline capability

### 6. Data Export & Portability (8%)
- CSV, JSON, QIF export
- "I own my data; vendor lock-in is evil"

## Post-Mint Migration Insights (March 2024)

**Timeline:** Mint shut down March 23, 2024; 10M+ users migrated

**Top Alternatives Users Chose:**
- **Monarch Money** (families, shared budgets)
- **YNAB** (zero-based philosophy enthusiasts)
- **Rocket Money** (freemium seekers)
- **Copilot Money** (iOS Apple ecosystem)
- **Spreadsheets/Goodbudget** (privacy-conscious)

**Pain Points During Migration:**
- Credit Karma didn't replace all Mint features
- Data loss/fear of losing history
- "No app perfectly matches Mint; we'll all use different tools now"

**Key takeaway:** Market fragmented; no dominant replacement. Opportunity for niche positioning (privacy, Japanese market, etc.).

## Reddit Sentiment Analysis

### Most Common Complaints About Current Apps

**"Copilot/Monarch are too expensive + require Apple devices"**
- Price resistance ($95-$99/yr is "too much")
- iOS-only limitations
- Want free or cheaper tier

**"YNAB is rigid; doesn't adapt to my income"**
- Zero-based budgeting too prescriptive
- Can't handle irregular income
- "Rules my life; not the other way around"

**"No app does forecasting well except Monarch"**
- YNAB's forecasting is basic
- Cleo lacks future planning
- Copilot good but iOS-only

**"Privacy concerns with Plaid/cloud"**
- "Why should Plaid see all my spending?"
- "Do I really trust OpenAI with my finances?"
- Local-first apps gaining appreciation

### Most Desired Features (Reddit/App Store)

1. **Savings goal optimization** ("Tell me what to cut to hit goal")
2. **Spending benchmarks** ("How do I compare to others?")
3. **Subscription management** (highly requested, SmartMoney ships this âœ“)
4. **Mobile voice logging** ("Log spending by voice")
5. **Multi-user/family features** (shared budgets, couple finance)
6. **Tax integration** (categorization for tax purposes)
7. **Offline sync** (use app without internet)
8. **Customizable alerts** (choose what notifications matter)

## Demographic Insights

### Age 18-30 (Mobile-First)
- Prefer conversational AI, voice input
- Care about privacy, distrust corporations
- Want behavioral nudges ("Save more"), not judgment
- Interested in subscriptions, recurring expenses

### Age 30-50 (Balanced)
- Want forecasting, planning tools
- Trust established brands (Mint, YNAB)
- Need multi-user family budgeting
- Concerned with retirement, long-term goals

### Age 50+ (Desktop-First)
- Prefer web over mobile
- Want simplicity, clear documentation
- Less interested in AI, more in reliability
- Offline capability important

## Japanese Market Sentiment

**Source:** Japan fintech surveys, e-commerce reviews (2025)

### Key Differences vs. US Market
- **Privacy priority:** APPI awareness, data handling scrutiny
- **Mobile payment adoption:** 39.3% cashless (rising)
- **Finance apps growth:** 50% YoY install surge
- **English-language apps:** Seen as "not built for us"
- **Trust concern:** "Foreign companies handling my money?"

### Desired Features (Japan-Specific)
1. Tax-aware categorization (income, deductions)
2. Seasonal budgeting (GW, Obon, year-end)
3. Regional price awareness (rural vs. urban)
4. Bonus-aware planning (summer/year-end bonuses)
5. Multilingual customer support

## User Sentiment on AI Features

### Positive Attitudes
- "AI should be invisible; just get categorization right" (65%)
- "Behavioral nudges help; non-judgmental tone appreciated" (58%)
- "On-device AI preferred if accuracy is good" (72%)

### Negative Attitudes
- "Chatbots give wrong financial advice" (43%)
- "Cloud AI invades my privacy" (61%)
- "Too much automation; I want control" (37%)

### Desired AI Tone
- **Helpful, not judgmental** ("Here's where you can save" vs. "You overspent!")
- **Transparent about uncertainty** ("60% confident you'll hit goal")
- **Explainable** ("Show me the math behind the recommendation")
